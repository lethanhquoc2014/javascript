1. \d Any digit from 0 to 9

Example: \d\d matches 72, but not aa or 7a

2. \D Any character that is not a digit

Example: \D\D\D matches abc, but not 123 or 8ef

3. \w Any word character; that is, A–Z, a–z, 0–9, and the underscore character (_)

Example: \w\w\w\w matches Ab_2, but not £$%* or Ab_@

4. \W Any non-word character

Example: \W\W matches @, but not a

5. \s Any whitespace character, including tab, newline, carriage return, formfeed, and vertical tab

Example: \s matches tab

6. \S Any non-whitespace character

Example: \S matches A, but not the tab character

7. . Any single character other than the newline character (\n)

Example: . matches a or 4 or @

8. [...] Any one of the characters between the brackets

Example: [abc] will match a or b or c, but nothing else
[a-z] will match any character in the range a to z

9. [^...] Any one character, but not one of those inside the brackets

Example: [^abc] will match any character except a or b or c
[^a-z] will match any character that is not in the range a to z
